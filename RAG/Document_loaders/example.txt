The Seven Powers — YC Edition (with Modern AI Examples)
YC used Hamilton Helmer’s book Seven Powers as the framework but reinterpreted it for today’s AI startups. Here’s my translated, plain-English version — with real companies that illustrate each moat.

1. Process Power — Mastering Complexity
This is when your product is so operationally or technically complex that it’s incredibly hard to copy.

YC cited CaseText, which built legal AI that lawyers could actually trust — not just a demo that sounds smart. It took years of refining the product to work reliably with real legal data and use cases.

Another example is Plaid, whose system connects to thousands of financial institutions. Anyone can build a prototype, but replicating Plaid’s depth and reliability would take a decade.

In AI, “process power” often looks like the unsexy engineering work — building thousands of edge cases, automation pipelines, and safety checks that no one sees but everyone depends on.
